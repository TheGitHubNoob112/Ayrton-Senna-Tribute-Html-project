Ayrton Senna

"The Rain Master"

Ayrton Senna at Interlagos in 1991.

1981

Senna Wins the British Formula Ford 1600 championship in his very first year racing cars.

1982

Wins both the British and European Formula Ford 2000 championships.

1983

Dominates British F3, earning a reputation as a rising superstar. and establishing himself as F1-ready.

1984

Debuts in Formula 1 with Toleman; shocks everyone with his monsoon drive at Monaco, nearly beating Alain Prost.

1985

Joins Lotus and takes his first F1 victory in Portugal, lapping the field in the wet – pure masterclass.

1986

Claims 2 more wins with Lotus and shows qualifying mastery.

1987

Wins 2 races, finishes 3rd in the Championship — his best result with Lotus.

1988

Moves to McLaren-Honda. Wins 8 races, including Monaco, and takes his first World Championship, beating teammate Prost.

1989

Fierce rivalry with Prost peaks. Collides at Suzuka, handing Prost the title. Finishes 2nd overall.

1990

Comes back stronger, winning 6 races. Another Suzuka clash with Prost seals Senna’s second World Championship.

1991

At his peak: wins 7 races and his third World Championship. His legendary victory at Brazil, finishing while stuck in 6th gear.

1992

McLaren falls behind Williams, but Senna still wins 3 races (including Monaco) and fights hard, finishing 4th overall.

1993

Despite inferior machinery, wins 5 races. His opening lap at Donington Park in the European GP is often called the greatest lap in F1 history. Finishes 2nd in the Championship.

1994

Joins Williams in search of a 4th title. Takes 3 poles in 3 races but suffers fatal crash at Imola’s San Marino GP, leading to sweeping F1 safety reforms.
